Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Our data analysis explored the increasing unaffordability of the Boston housing market, the influence of growing investments, the profit margin disparity between luxury and non-luxury housing, and the corporate ownership of luxury properties. The investigation began with addressing potential data quality issues, such as focusing on certain property types and specific regions of the market. Temporal and spatial analyses revealed a clear increase in investment activity, with a shift towards hi-rise and luxury housing for investment purposes. The visualizations confirmed the influence of investment on housing prices, showing a positive correlation between the investment transactions and average prices. The investigation then shifts focus to the variation in profit margins, demonstrating that luxury housing consistently exhibited larger margins than non-luxury housing. The investigation concludes by highlighting a growing trend of corporate ownership in Boston's luxury high-rises, indicating increasing corporate investments in this sector.
